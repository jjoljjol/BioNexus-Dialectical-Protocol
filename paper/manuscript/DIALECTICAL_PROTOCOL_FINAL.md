## Abstract
This paper introduces the **Dialectical Protocol**, a multi-agent framework for autonomous AI evolution inspired by biological immune systems and DNA proofreading. We address the fundamental "Proofreading Deficiency" in current Large Language Models (LLMs) by transitioning toward a specialized multi-agent architecture capable of systemic self-audit. Central to this framework is **Vibe Coding**---a methodology that leverages high-dimensional biological metaphors as executable functional specifications, bridging the gap between natural language guidance and rigorous code synthesis. Through a triple-layered architecture comprising **Genetic Proofreading** (Micro-level), **Dialectical Immunity** (Macro-level), and **Merkle Checkpointing** (Safety), we demonstrate that agentic systems can autonomously resolve logic conflicts in complex multi-omics datasets with a 94% success rate---outperforming traditional prompting by 26%. Our results, highlighted by the "Generation 14" autonomous discovery of Bayesian logic, provide a robust pathway for safe, self-correcting scientific intelligence.

## 1. Introduction
Current artificial intelligence research has primarily focused on *Neural Mimicry*---the attempt to replicate biological neural structures through deep learning architectures [Vaswani et al. 2017, Brown et al. 2020]. While successful, this paradigm remains static, lacking the autonomous error-correction and evolutionary adaptability seen in complex biological systems, such as the rapid regenerative capacity of echinoderms like *Apostichopus japonicus* [Zhang et al. 2017, Sun et al. 2019]. We identify this lack of feedback-driven repair as a fundamental **Proofreading Deficiency**, leading to stochastic failures commonly known as AI hallucinations.

In this paper, we propose a transition toward an **Organism-mimetic AI architecture**. We treat hallucinations not as mere statistical anomalies, but as *Digital Pathogenesis*---pathological drifts that require a systemic immune response. 

Our core methodology, **Vibe Coding**, allows for the translation of high-level biological metaphors into executable code, enabling a multi-agent adversarial committee to audit, repair, and evolve the system's own logic.

## 2. Related Work
Existing frameworks like **AutoGPT** [Significant Gravitas 2023] and **LangGraph** [LangChain AI 2023] focus on iterative refinement or static task graphs. However, they lack an adversarial validation layer and the ability to structurally evolve their own logic over generations without human intervention. Recent advancements in **Agentic Workflows** [Ng 2024] emphasize design patterns, while **RL-PPO** based methods optimize for narrow reward functions [Oyang et al. 2022].

While **Genetic Algorithms** [Goldberg 1989] and **Artificial Immune Systems (AIS)** [De Castro & Timmis 2002, Dasgupta et al. 2011] have been long established, they typically rely on random bit-wise mutations. Stephanie Forrest's pioneering work on self-nonself discrimination [Forrest et al. 1994] provides the conceptual basis for our Digital Thymus, yet our approach introduces semantic understanding through LLM-driven "T-Cell" audits.

## 3. Methodology: Vibe Coding
Traditional code synthesis relies on formal logic or rigid prompt engineering. We introduce **Vibe Coding**---a methodology where high-dimensional biological metaphors serve as executable functional specifications.

**Definition (Vibe Coding):**
Let $\mathcal{V}$ be a high-dimensional vibe space and $\mathcal{M}$ a metaphor vocabulary derived from biological functional archetypes. Vibe Coding is a mapping:
$$\phi: \mathcal{V} \times \mathcal{M} \rightarrow \mathcal{C}$$
where $\mathcal{C}$ is executable code.

Unlike traditional prompt engineering which specifies *how* a computation should proceed, Vibe Coding specifies the *biological objective*, aligning with the recent paradigm of intent-based programming [Karpathy 2025, Sørensen et al. 2024].
