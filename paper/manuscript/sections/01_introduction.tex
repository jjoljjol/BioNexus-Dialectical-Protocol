Current artificial intelligence research has primarily focused on \textit{Neural Mimicry}---the attempt to replicate biological neural structures through deep learning architectures \cite{vaswani2017attention, brown2020language}. While successful, this paradigm remains static, lacking the autonomous error-correction and evolutionary adaptability seen in complex biological systems, such as the rapid regenerative capacity of echinoderms like \textit{Apostichopus japonicus} \cite{zhang2017cucumbergenome, sun2019wnt}. We identify this lack of feedback-driven repair as a fundamental \textbf{Proofreading Deficiency}, leading to stochastic failures commonly known as AI hallucinations.

In this paper, we propose a transition toward an \textbf{Organism-mimetic AI architecture}. We treat hallucinations not as mere statistical anomalies, but as \textit{Digital Pathogenesis}---pathological drifts that require a systemic immune response. 

Our core methodology, \textbf{Vibe Coding}, allows for the translation of high-level biological metaphors into executable code, enabling a multi-agent adversarial committee to audit, repair, and evolve the system's own logic. Our contributions include:
\begin{enumerate}
    \item A formal definition of the Holistic AI Organism paradigm.
    \item The Dialectical Protocol: a multi-agent adversarial evolution framework.
    \item Vibe Coding: a natural-language substrate for rigorous code synthesis.
    \item Empirical validation demonstrating 0\% catastrophic drift and 94\% logic resolution in multi-omics datasets.
\end{enumerate}
